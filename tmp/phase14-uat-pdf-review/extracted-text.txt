   FALSE COMFORT




Where a strong average can still hide a real gap
A domain score is an average. It can still contain one specific control that is materially weaker than the
others feeding into the same number. The purpose of this report is to surface that unevenness so
leadership does not treat the overall score as a substitute for control-level attention.

   METHODOLOGY AND LIMITATIONS




How this reading was produced
This report is generated from a structured self-assessment across ten areas of fraud risk management,
each weighted by relative importance to overall fraud readiness. Certain control failures can limit the
overall reading even where other areas score well.



Limitations

This is a structured self-assessment, not a forensic investigation, external audit or compliance
certification. It reflects responses at a point in time and should be read as a diagnostic starting point, not
assurance that fraud has not occurred or cannot occur.
